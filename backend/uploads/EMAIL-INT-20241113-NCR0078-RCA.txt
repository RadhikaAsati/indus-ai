Dear Mr. Iyer,

Please find below my findings from the root cause analysis for NCR-2024-0078
(surface finish non-conformance on batch PL2-NOV-042). This is also relevant
to the customer complaint CC-2024-0047 received from Kiran Pumps on 11-Nov.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS SUMMARY (5-Why Method):

Problem: Surface finish (Ra) and flatness out of specification on batches
         PL2-NOV-041 and PL2-NOV-042 (Pump Body PB-7, Pump Line 2).

Why 1: Why was surface finish out of spec?
  — Insufficient pressing pressure during forming operation on HP-05.

Why 2: Why was pressing pressure insufficient?
  — HP-05 was operating at 206–210 bar actual (vs. required 220 bar) for
    a period estimated at 2–4 weeks prior to correction on 05-Nov-2024.
    (Pressure relief valve was found set at 208 bar on 05-Nov. Gauge reads
    ~2 bar high per calibration CAL-2024-0156.)

Why 3: Why was the pressure relief valve set lower than specification?
  — Under investigation. Rajesh Sharma has confirmed no documented adjustment
    was made. Operators on shift were informally asked — no one admits to
    adjusting it. Valve drift alone seems unlikely at this magnitude.
    (Note: valve drift was also not observed in Oct PM — Oct PM noted valve at
    correct 220 bar setting. This means drift or adjustment occurred between
    07-Oct and 05-Nov.)

Why 4: Why was the low pressure not detected earlier?
  — The pressure drop was gradual. Operators noticed fluctuation only on
    04-Nov. There is no automated alarm on HP-05 for pressure deviation
    from set point. Operator visual checks of the pressure gauge are not
    part of the formal pre-shift checklist.

Why 5: Why is there no automated alarm or formal gauge check procedure?
  — HP-05 is an older press (installed 2017). No automated monitoring
    was retrofitted. Pre-shift checklist does not include pressure gauge
    verification.

ROOT CAUSE (Primary): Pressure relief valve operating below required setting
  for approximately 2–4 weeks, resulting in under-pressed components with
  surface finish and flatness deviations. Exact cause of valve setting change
  remains UNCONFIRMED — either undocumented manual adjustment or accelerated
  valve seat wear.

CONTRIBUTING FACTOR: Absence of formal pressure gauge check in operator
  pre-shift inspection, allowing low pressure condition to persist undetected.

100% INSPECTION RESULT (Batch PL2-NOV-042):
